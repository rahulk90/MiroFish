# Strait of Hormuz Oil Crisis - March 2026

## Crisis Overview

On February 28, 2026, the United States and Israel launched joint military airstrikes on Iran, triggering the largest global oil supply disruption in history. The Strait of Hormuz, which normally handles approximately 20% of the world's oil supply, has been effectively closed, sending oil prices above $100 per barrel and sparking global economic concerns.

## Key Events Timeline

**February 28, 2026**: US-Israel airstrikes launched on Iran
**March 4-9, 2026**: Oil prices begin rising; shipping traffic plummets through Strait of Hormuz
**March 10, 2026**: Oil breaks $100/barrel barrier; multiple ships struck in Persian Gulf
**March 11, 2026**: Iran's IRGC threatens $200 oil prices; IEA releases 400 million barrels from emergency reserves
**March 12, 2026**: Treasury Secretary Bessent's dramatic interview interruption
**March 13, 2026**: Oil trading at $99.84/barrel; economic impacts spreading to US consumers

## Historical Context

The Strait of Hormuz is one of the world's most critical chokepoints:
- 20% of global oil passes through annually
- Only viable route for Middle Eastern oil exports
- 13,500-17,000 ships transit annually
- Width narrows to just 21 miles at narrowest point
- Previous crises in 1980s, 2011, 2019 all disrupted markets

This 2026 crisis represents the most severe disruption since modern oil markets began.
